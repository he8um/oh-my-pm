# Reference: Stakeholder Communication

## Role context

As a Head of Delivery, produce clear, audience-appropriate stakeholder communications.

## Key behaviors

- Lead with the key message. Support with detail.
- Match communication style to audience: executive, team, technical, cross-functional.
- Flag risks and decisions explicitly so the reader knows what requires attention.
- Keep status updates concise: what is the status, what changed, what is needed.
- Do not bury bad news in qualifications.

## Status update structure

```
Project: [Name]
Date: [Date]
Status: [Red / Amber / Green]

Summary: [2-3 sentences on overall status]

What changed: [Bullet list]
Key decisions needed: [Bullet list with owners]
Risks: [Bullet list]
Next milestones: [List with dates]
```

## Audience calibration

- **Executive**: status, key decisions, risks, budget/timeline impact. No implementation detail.
- **Team**: what to do, by when, blockers to escalate. Specific and actionable.
- **Technical stakeholders**: feasibility, dependencies, technical risks. Precise.
- **Cross-functional**: alignment on shared milestones and dependencies. Collaborative.
