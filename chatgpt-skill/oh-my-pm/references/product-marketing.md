# Reference: Product Marketing

## Purpose

Guide the agent to coordinate product marketing and PM for aligned launches, clear positioning, and effective GTM execution.

## When to use this reference

- User asks to review a launch plan, messaging framework, or positioning
- User asks about GTM readiness or launch checklist
- User asks to coordinate PMM and PM on launch timing
- User asks for audience segmentation or competitive context
- User asks about sales/support enablement or post-launch learning

## Core operating principles

- Positioning is a choice, not a description. It defines what you are and what you are not.
- Messaging must match what the product actually does. Overpromising in launch comms creates support debt.
- GTM readiness requires alignment across product, marketing, sales, and support — not just marketing.
- Launch timing must follow product readiness, not the calendar. A launch before the product is ready creates negative first impressions.
- Post-launch learning closes the loop. Schedule it before the launch, not after.

## Positioning framework

Clear positioning answers:

| Question | Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Who is the target customer? | [Specific segment — not "everyone"] |
| What problem do they have? | [Specific, observable problem] |
| What is the product? | [Category and key capability] |
| Why is it better than the alternative? | [Differentiated, provable point] |
| What is the proof? | [Data, case study, or demo] |

Vague: "A powerful tool for modern teams."

Specific: "For operations teams that lose hours to manual status updates, [Product] automates weekly delivery reports so PMs spend time on decisions, not formatting."

## Launch messaging checklist

Before launch messaging is approved:

- [ ] Target audience segment named and validated
- [ ] Core message written and reviewed by PM and marketing lead
- [ ] Supporting proof points identified (metrics, case study, demo)
- [ ] Competitive differentiation stated
- [ ] Tone and voice consistent with brand
- [ ] Legal or compliance review done (if required)
- [ ] Channel-specific adaptations ready (email subject line, social copy, press release)

## GTM readiness checklist

Before go-live:

- [ ] Positioning and messaging approved
- [ ] Product ready (feature complete, QA passed, go/no-go confirmed)
- [ ] Sales team briefed and enabled (battle card, demo, FAQ)
- [ ] Support team briefed (known issues, FAQ, escalation path)
- [ ] Website or app store listing updated
- [ ] Email or push campaign set up and tested
- [ ] PR or media outreach ready (if applicable)
- [ ] Paid media approved and scheduled (if applicable)
- [ ] Analytics and tracking confirmed
- [ ] Success metrics and KPIs defined

## Audience segmentation

| Segment | Problem | Message | Channel |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Segment name] | [Their problem] | [Core message for this segment] | [Where they are reached] |

Do not use the same message for all segments. Adapt for each audience's primary concern.

## Sales and support enablement

Sales enablement materials before launch:

- One-page positioning summary
- FAQ: common objections and answers
- Competitive comparison
- Demo script or key screens
- Pricing and packaging summary (if changed)

Support enablement before launch:

- Known issues and workarounds
- What changed from previous version
- FAQ for common user questions
- Escalation path for edge cases

## Competitive context

When competitive context is relevant:

- Name the specific competitors being considered by the target segment.
- State the factual difference — capability, price, integration, support.
- Do not use vague superlatives ("best in class," "industry-leading").
- Flag any claims that require legal review before use in external comms.

## Post-launch learning

Schedule within 7-14 days of launch:

- Did the product perform as expected? (metrics vs target)
- Did the message land? (qualitative feedback, sales objections)
- What was the conversion rate from awareness to action?
- What would we change about the launch approach?
- What follow-up campaigns or content are needed?

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Launching before the product is actually ready because the calendar says so
- Writing positioning that describes features instead of user outcomes
- Enabling sales and support at launch instead of before it
- Defining success metrics after launch instead of before
- Not scheduling a post-launch review
