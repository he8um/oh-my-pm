# Example: Marketing Project — Output (English)

This is a synthetic example of Oh My PM Head of Delivery output.

---

## Campaign diagnosis: Q3 mobile app v3.0 launch

**Status:** 🟡 Amber

Creative asset production is behind due to designer bandwidth constraints. The app store submission window depends on final product screenshots, which are unavailable until feature freeze in two weeks. At current pace, the submission window is less than 3 days before the target launch, below the typical 1-3 day review time.

---

## Critical path

1. Designer completes launch-critical creative assets — Week 3
2. Feature freeze → screenshots taken → app store listing complete — Week 4
3. App store submission — Week 4 (no buffer to hit target launch in Week 6)
4. Platform review (1-3 days) — Week 4-5
5. Press outreach and social content finalized — Week 5
6. Campaign live — Week 6

**Buffer: 2 days.** This assumes designer delivers on time and the app store review takes 1 day.

---

## Top risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Designer bottleneck delays all creative | High | High | Assign designer to launch-critical assets only. Defer secondary assets. Identify freelance backup now. | PMM lead |
| Screenshot dependency delays app store submission | High | High | Book screenshot session for day 1 of feature freeze. Draft listing copy now — add screenshots when available. | PMM |
| Platform review takes 2-3 days, not 1 | Medium | High | Submit by end of Week 4 (not Week 5). Do not schedule campaign live for the same day as submission. | PMM |
| Measurement not in place at launch | Medium | High | Confirm UTM parameters and analytics events are in the engineering sprint. Verify before launch. | PM |
| Success metric (10,000 installs) has no baseline | Low | Medium | Confirm historical install rate for comparable campaigns before Week 4. | PMM |

## Post-launch review

Schedule a 30-minute post-launch review 2 weeks after launch to assess:
- Install rate vs 10,000 target
- Top-performing channel (organic, paid, press)
- Creative assets that drove the most conversions
